About Clinical Classification

A brain tumor is a mass or growth of abnormal cells. Fast, automated, and accurate detection guides initial treatment directions. This system classifies T1-weighted Contrast-Enhanced MRI (CE-MRI) images into Glioma, Meningioma, Pituitary, or No Tumor (None).

Common Indications & Symptoms

Signs vary depending on the tumor's size, location, and rate of growth. Common symptoms include:

Severe Headaches Seizures / Convulsions Speech / Word-finding Difficulties Cognitive & Personality Changes Weakness or Numbness Balance Loss & Dizziness Vision & Hearing Changes
